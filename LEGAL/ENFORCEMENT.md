# RCF-ENFORCEMENT — Legal Procedures

**Version:** Active  
**Status:** Active

## 1. Violation Detection

Violations may be identified through the tooling RCF actually ships — `rcf-cli verify` / `rcf-cli diff` against a published `RCF-AUDIT-REPORT.json`, and the correlation/surprisal analysis in `rcf_core` when run against a specific suspected codebase — combined with manual monitoring (repository audits, commercial product analysis). RCF does not run continuous forensic logging or behavioral-analysis monitoring; detection requires a human to run these tools against a specific target.

## 2. Enforcement Workflow

1. **Evidence Collection:** Capturing logs, session data, and documented proof of unauthorized replication.
2. **Notice of Termination:** Serving notice that, under RCF-PL's terms, rights are asserted to have terminated automatically upon violation. As described in LEGAL.md §6, whether a violation in fact occurred — and therefore whether termination was actually triggered — is a question a court resolves if disputed; this notice states RCF-PL's position, not an adjudicated outcome.
3. **Cease and Desist:** Issuing formal demands to stop unauthorized use.
4. **Legal Remedy:** Pursuing damages for copyright infringement and breach of contract.

## 3. Damages

The Author reserves the right to seek:
- Actual damages and profits gained by the infringer.
- Statutory damages where applicable.
- Injunctive relief to prevent further use of the Protected Work.

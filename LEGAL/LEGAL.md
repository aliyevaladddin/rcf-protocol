## 1. Legal Basis of RCF

The Restricted Correlation Framework (RCF) is based on international copyright law and contract law. It operates under the principle that the author of a work has the exclusive right to define the conditions of its use.

### 1.1 Copyright Protection
RCF-protected works are subject to protection under the Berne Convention and national copyright laws. The RCF-PL v1.1 license serves as a conditional grant of visibility rights.

### 1.2 Contractual Nature
By accessing or using RCF-protected materials, the User enters into a binding agreement with the Author. Usage beyond the defined "Visibility" rights constitutes a breach of contract.

## 2. Visibility vs. Usage Rights

| Right Type | Definition | Status in RCF |
|------------|------------|---------------|
| **Visibility** | The right to read, study, and audit the work manually. | Granted automatically. |
| **Usage** | The right to implement, replicate, or automate extraction. | Restricted (requires authorization). |

## 3. Prohibited Activities

1. **Automated Extraction:** Any harvesting of code or data bypassing human-readability constraints.
2. **AI/ML Training:** Using the work to train, fine-tune, or validate artificial intelligence models.
3. **Methodology Replication:** Implementing the core algorithms or correlation logic in other projects without permission.
4. **Commercial Exploitation:** Using the work for direct or indirect financial gain.

## 4. Termination of Rights

All rights granted under RCF are **automatically and immediately terminated** upon any violation. There is no requirement for a "cure period" or prior notice.
